Lemon Garlic Chicken
Dish Description

Lemon garlic chicken is a popular dish that combines tender chicken with a flavorful sauce made from lemon and garlic. The chicken is usually sautéed or roasted and then served with a sauce that is made from fresh lemon juice, minced garlic, olive oil, and herbs such as parsley and basil. The sauce is often used to baste the chicken as it cooks, infusing it with the bright and tangy flavors of lemon and garlic. The finished dish is typically served with sides such as rice, roasted vegetables, or a fresh salad, and is a great option for a weeknight dinner or a casual dinner party. The lemon garlic flavor profile is bright, fresh, and savory, making it a popular choice for many home cooks and diners alike.

Ingredients
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 3 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • 1 ½ teaspoons ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons garlic powder, divided
  • 1 lemon, juiced
Cooking Directions
  1. Melt butter in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Season chicken with salt and pepper; place in melted butter. Cook chicken, flipping frequently, until browned, about 5 minutes. Sprinkle 1 tablespoon garlic powder over chicken; cook for 2 minutes. Flip and sprinkle remaining 1 tablespoon garlic powder on the second side; cook for 2 minutes.
  3. Pour lemon juice over each side of chicken and cook until no longer pink in the center, 5 to 10 minutes more. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 165 degrees F (74 degrees C).
